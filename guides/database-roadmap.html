Database roadmap

How This Becomes a Complete Subnautica 2 Player Database

The site should feel ambitious without pretending unfinished Early Access data is already known. This roadmap shows what is ready, what is being verified, what players can submit, and what stays unpublished until evidence exists.

Current Database State

StateContent lanesPlayer value
Ready nowDownload/install, Game Pass, system requirements, settings, crash triage, co-op setup, save safety, beginner route, update tracking.Helps a player decide where to play, launch the game, fix urgent problems, and start safely.
Structured but data-lightResources, blueprints, map/biomes, base planning, character comparison, creatures, tools, vehicles, story expansion.Shows exactly where future game data will live and what evidence each row needs.
Ready for submissionsScreenshots, build showcases, co-op tests, bug evidence, ambience notes, resource/blueprint/map reports.Turns player discoveries into useful guide updates without copying content or losing source context.
Later platform workReal forum posts, accounts, comments, uploads, moderation queue, voting, user profiles.Useful only after spam control, moderation, privacy, and content ownership rules are ready.

Content Expansion Board

P0: Player accessKeep download, install, specs, settings, crashes, Game Pass, and co-op current because these solve urgent searches.
P1: Play progressionFill resources, blueprints, map routes, base planning, and beginner-to-midgame workflows as evidence arrives.
P2: World depthBuild creature, vehicle, tool, character, story, music, screenshot, and build showcase lanes with spoiler controls.
P3: Community layerMove from structured reports to real discussion only after moderation, anti-spam, and privacy are solved.
Always: Source labelsEvery claim should say whether it is official, directly tested, external-guide reported, community reported, or unverified.
Never: Fake completionNo invented map, resource, blueprint, creature, room cost, character stat, unlock chain, or story detail.

Not Published Until Proven

Some topics are exactly what players want, but they also create the biggest trust risk. These stay out of finished database rows until the evidence is strong.

TopicWhy blockedUnlock condition
Character abilities or stat differencesPlayers may assume one character is better if the site invents differences.Official confirmation or repeatable current-build tests.
Creature behavior and locationsHigh spoiler risk and high chance of patch changes.Official names, direct sightings, screenshots, and spoiler labels.
Full visual mapA fake map is worse than no map.Enough verified routes, official names, landmarks, and patch stability.
Tool and vehicle unlock chainsBad unlock data wastes player time.Exact names, unlock source, requirements, screenshots, and build date.
Story summariesSpoilers and Early Access changes need careful handling.Official patch notes or direct play evidence with spoiler-light and spoiler-heavy separation.
Player mediaCopyright and credit matter.Original file or permitted link, explicit permission, credit, platform, build date, spoiler level.
